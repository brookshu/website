Northwestern Global Health Technologies Program: South Africa 1/2026 – 3/2026

During winter quarter of my senior year, I studied abroad at Stellenbosch University in South Africa as I participated in the Northwestern Global Health Technologies Program. I worked on a project focused on developing an automated chest X-ray quality assurance system, which used YOLOv11 to segment important anatomical structures that are used to measure rotation and inspiration of the patient.

This project was focused on the local region, which is plagued by a high incidence of tuberculosis and a shortage of radiographers and radiologists in the public sector, which is incredibly disadvantaged compared to the private sector. We acquired data from Tygerberg Hospital, which is the second largest hospital in South Africa, and local to Cape Town.

Northwestern Formula SAE 9/2022 – 12/2025

As part of the chassis subteam, I designed and machined brake and throttle pedals, along with the pedal faces, using Solidworks and 7075-T6 aluminum. We used CNC machines and drill presses to fabricate the parts, and I performed Finite Element Analysis in Solidworks to confirm that the pedals could withstand forces of 2000N for the brake and 1000N for the throttle. As a member of the pedal box team, I helped integrate the pedals with the base plate and played a crucial role in the transition to EV by incorporating sensors to measure pedal rotation.

Afterwards, I transitioned to the data acquisition subteam (AKA "DAQ") as part of the wheel and suspension monitoring group to gain more real-world experience related to my major. I created schematics in Altium for a monitoring board inside the interior of the wheels, which tracked wheel speed, suspension strain, and suspension displacement. Our other board sensed wheel temperature and was mounted above the wheel, with thermocouples pointed at the wheel. After designing the schematics, I soldered the components onto the board, flashed them, and helped with testing and debugging.

NBA data modeling 1/2025 - 3/2025

This project analyzes NBA player and team performance using data from NBA-API, a comprehensive database accessible via RapidAPI. By leveraging detailed statistics from two seasons, including player-specific game data and team performance metrics, the analysis focused on identifying trends and answering targeted questions about game outcomes and player impact between the first and second halves of the regular season.

AWS Speech Analysis 9/2024 - 12/2024

Developed an AWS-based server/client system for real-time processing of audio files, enabling transcription, word count, and character count analysis. The client, a Python script running in a terminal, operates in an infinite loop to accept .wav and .mp4 files, send them to the server, and retrieve processed results. The server, deployed on AWS, utilizes services like AWS S3 for file storage and AWS Transcribe for speech-to-text conversion.

Mass Flow Control System Design 9/2021 – 6/2022

I led a research project to design a mass flow control system for circulating antifreeze to cool machines at user-specified flow rates. I worked on integrating key components, including pumps, a flow speed sensor, and a cold plate to ensure the system's robustness and effectiveness. I wired and programmed pumps and sensors using an Arduino, implementing the system in C++. The control loop dynamically adjusted pump speeds based on real-time data from flow sensors, ensuring accurate and reliable operation. To optimize the system's performance, I applied control theory techniques, including tuning proportional and integral variables to manage system response and dealt with issues such as integrator windup. I utilized Simulink to model and simulate the control loop, allowing for precise adjustments to how the system reacted to changes in flow rate. The finalized control loop allowed for users to dynamically adjust pump speeds based on real-time data from the flow sensor, ensuring accurate and reliable operation.

This system served as a validation tool for thermal testbed components and provided reproducible wiring designs and code for researchers. Additionally, I delivered two research presentations to POETS staff, including professors, technicians, and graduate students at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign.

Coolstem YouTube 4/2020 – 3/2022

In high school, I leveraged my skills in Adobe Premiere Pro to produce a series of short, educational YouTube videos with a friend, exploring scientific concepts in an engaging way. The topics ranged from M&M diffusion and the principles behind fireworks to the mechanics of beach balls floating on leaf blowers, with plans to cover lenses, the Magnus effect, compasses, and heat-resistant tiles for spacecraft. I handled all aspects of video production, including filming, editing, and visual storytelling, while my friend focused on scriptwriting and on-camera presentation. These videos blended clear explanations of phenomena, like the polarity of water molecules, diffusion, and Bernoulli’s principle, with creative visuals, making complex concepts accessible and enjoyable for viewers. This project highlights my passion for videography, storytelling, and education through multimedia.
